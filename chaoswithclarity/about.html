The longer story

I started writing because the spreadsheets stopped making sense. Not financially — the numbers were always there. But existentially. You can model a company's runway to the day. You cannot model why you lie awake at 2am wondering if any of it means anything.

Nine years of finance — from audit rooms at EY to sitting across from founders who had everything and felt nothing — taught me one thing: clarity without depth is just noise with good formatting.

The poems started in a notebook. Then they started helping people. Not because they had answers, but because they named things that people had been carrying silently.

Chaos With Clarity is where I bring both sides together. The CFO who can read your runway and the poet who can sit with what the runway is actually for.

If that's the conversation you've been looking for — you're in the right place.
